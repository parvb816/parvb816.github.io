Real impact

I fixed a bug that was crashing the call system under load.

When the dialer got busy, calls started failing. I traced the cause, redesigned how the background tasks take turns, and the failures stopped. Toggle below to see the before and after.

● Calls failing — two tasks stuck on each other

Each task grabbed a record the other one needed, so both froze waiting — and calls started dropping whenever the system got busy.

What I actually did

// from the live dialer system
01
Found the pattern. The failures only happened when lots of calls were running at once — a classic sign that two background tasks were colliding.
02
Made them take turns. I redesigned the tasks so they always reach for records in the same order. That way one simply waits instead of clashing — the freeze can't happen.
03
Added a safety net. If two tasks ever do conflict, the system now quietly retries on its own instead of dropping a call.
→
The result: calls stopped failing under load, and the queue stayed reliable even on the busiest days.
For engineers: MySQL deadlock on the jobs table, fixed with consistent lock ordering (lockForUpdate), DB::transaction() retries, and Cache::lock() for job uniqueness.
